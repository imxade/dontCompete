**Properties of Language**
=========================

### Introduction
The properties of language are a fundamental concept in the theory of computation, which deals with the study of algorithms and their complexity. This topic focuses on the characteristics of languages, including regular languages, context-free languages, and recursively enumerable languages.

### Core Concepts
#### Regular Languages
A regular language is a language that can be accepted by a finite automaton (FA). Regular languages have certain properties:

*   **Closure under union**: The union of two regular languages is also regular.
*   **Closure under concatenation**: The concatenation of two regular languages is also regular.
*   **Closure under Kleene star**: The Kleene star of a regular language is also regular.

#### Context-Free Languages
A context-free language is a language that can be generated by a context-free grammar (CFG). Context-free languages have certain properties:

*   **Regular subsets are closed**: If a context-free language has a subset that is regular, then the entire language is regular.
*   **Deterministic finite automata (DFA) vs. nondeterministic finite automata (NFA)**: DFAs recognize only regular languages, while NFAs can recognize non-regular languages.

#### Recursively Enumerable Languages
A recursively enumerable language is a language that can be recognized by a Turing machine (TM). Recursively enumerable languages have certain properties:

*   **Recursion theorem**: If a recursively enumerable language has a subset that is recursive, then the entire language is recursive.
*   **Reduction to other problems**: Recursively enumerable languages can often be reduced to simpler problems.

### Examples with Solutions
**Example 1:** Let $L_1$ be a regular language, and let $L_3$ be a non-regular language. Which of the following statements is/are always TRUE?

(A) $\{x | x \in L_1^2\} = L_1$ if and only if $L_1^2 = \emptyset$

(B) $L_1 \cap L_3$ is not regular

(C) $L_3$ is not regular

(D) $\{x | x \in L_1^2\}$ is regular

**Solution:** Based on the properties of regular languages, we know that:

*   The union and concatenation of regular languages are also regular.
*   However, the intersection of a regular language with a non-regular language may not be regular.

Therefore, statements (B) and (C) are always true. Statement (A) is false because $L_1^2$ can be regular even if $L_1$ is regular. Statement (D) is also false because $\{x | x \in L_1^2\}$ may not be regular.
