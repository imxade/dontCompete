**Spatial Aptitude**
======================

**Introduction**
---------------

Spatial aptitude is the ability to reason about spatial relationships and visualize objects in space. It involves understanding how shapes, patterns, and objects interact with each other. This topic requires a combination of logic, visualization, and problem-solving skills.

**Core Concepts**
-----------------

### 1. Basic Shapes and Symmetry

*   A shape has rotational symmetry if it looks the same after rotating by a certain angle.
*   A shape has reflection symmetry if it looks the same when reflected over a line.

### 2. Spatial Reasoning

*   The ability to visualize objects in space and understand their relationships.
*   Understanding how shapes fit together, such as nesting or tangling.

### 3. Logic and Patterns

*   Using logical reasoning to solve spatial problems.
*   Recognizing patterns and applying them to new situations.

### Example 1: Question 63 (cs_2022_63)

The corners and midpoints of a triangle are labeled using distinct letters, not necessarily in the same order. Consider the following statements:

*   The line joining P and R is parallel to the line joining Q and S.
*   P is placed on the side opposite to the corner T.
*   S and U cannot be placed in the same side.

Which one of the following statements is correct based on the above information?

```solution
Step 1: Visualize the problem
We have a triangle with labeled corners and midpoints. We need to determine which statement is correct.

Step 2: Analyze each statement
*   The line joining P and R is parallel to the line joining Q and S. This can be true, as opposite sides of a triangle are parallel.
*   P is placed on the side opposite to the corner T. This must be true, as P cannot be on the same side as T.
*   S and U cannot be placed in the same side. This must also be true, as they represent midpoints and cannot coincide.

Step 3: Determine the correct answer
From our analysis, we see that all three statements are true. However, only one option is provided as the correct answer. Based on the question's context, the correct answer is (C) S cannot be placed at a corner.
```

### Example 2:

A figure consists of two identical squares with sides of length $s$ and two identical rectangles with dimensions $r \times s$. The perimeter of the figure can be expressed in terms of $s$ and $r$.

```solution
Step 1: Analyze the problem
We have a figure consisting of two squares and two rectangles. We need to find the perimeter of this figure in terms of the given variables.

Step 2: Calculate the perimeter
The perimeter consists of four sides from the squares (each of length $s$) and two longer sides from the rectangles (each of length $r + s$). So, the total perimeter can be expressed as $4s + 2(r+s)$.

Step 3: Simplify the expression
We can simplify this by combining like terms to get $6s + 2r$.
```
