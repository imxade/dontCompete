# Powder Metallurgy
=====================================

## Introduction
---------------

Powder metallurgy (PM) is a process for creating metal parts from metal powders. It involves compacting and sintering the powder to form the desired shape and properties. PM offers several advantages over traditional casting and machining methods, including improved mechanical properties, reduced material waste, and increased design flexibility.

## Core Concepts
----------------

### Powder Characteristics

*   **Powder size distribution**: The size of powder particles is critical in determining the final product's properties.
*   **Powder shape**: Spherical or irregular shapes can affect packing density and sintering behavior.
*   **Powder composition**: Alloying elements can enhance mechanical properties.

### Compaction and Sintering

*   **Compaction**: Powder is pressed into a compact using various techniques (e.g., uniaxial pressing, cold isostatic pressing).
*   **Sintering**: The compact is heated to a temperature below the melting point of the material to promote atomic diffusion and bonding.

### Key Formulas/Theorems

$$ \rho = \frac{m}{V} $$

Where:

*   $\rho$ is the density
*   $m$ is the mass
*   $V$ is the volume

$$ E = mc^2 $$

However, this formula is not directly applicable to PM. Instead, we focus on:

$$ T_s = T + \frac{v^2}{2c_p} $$

Where:

*   $T_s$ is the stagnation temperature
*   $T$ is the static temperature
*   $v$ is the velocity
*   $c_p$ is the specific heat capacity at constant pressure

### Example 2: Stagnation Temperature

Given:

*   Velocity $v = 300$ m/s
*   Static temperature $T = 330$ K
*   Specific heat capacity at constant pressure $c_p = 1.005$ kJ/kgK

Determine the stagnation temperature:

$$ T_s = T + \frac{v^2}{2c_p} $$

$$ T_s = 330 + \frac{(300)^2}{2(1.005)} $$

$$ T_s ≈ 374.77 K $$
