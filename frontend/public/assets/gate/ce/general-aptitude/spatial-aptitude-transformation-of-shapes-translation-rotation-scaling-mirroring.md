**Spatial Aptitude: Transformation of Shapes**
=============================================

**Introduction**
---------------

Spatial aptitude tests an individual's ability to visualize and manipulate shapes, understand spatial relationships, and recognize patterns. This topic focuses on transformations of shapes, including translation, rotation, scaling, mirroring, assembling, grouping, paper folding, cutting, and patterns in 2D and 3D.

**Core Concepts**
-----------------

### 1. Translation

*   Moving a shape from one position to another without rotating or scaling it.
*   Example: Shifting a square from (x1, y1) to (x2, y2).

    \[ T = \begin{bmatrix} x_2 \\ y_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 + t_x \\ y_1 + t_y \end{bmatrix} \]

### 2. Rotation

*   Rotating a shape around a fixed point or axis without scaling it.
*   Example: Rotating a circle by 90° clockwise.

    \[ R = \begin{bmatrix} x' \\ y' \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} x \cos(\theta) - y \sin(\theta) \\ x \sin(\theta) + y \cos(\theta) \end{bmatrix} \]

### 3. Scaling

*   Enlarging or shrinking a shape by a certain factor.
*   Example: Scaling a rectangle to twice its size.

    \[ S = k \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} kx \\ ky \end{bmatrix} \]

### 4. Mirroring

*   Reflecting a shape across a line or plane.
*   Example: Mirroring a square across its horizontal axis.

    \[ M = \begin{bmatrix} x' \\ y' \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -x \\ y \end{bmatrix} \]

### 5. Assembling and Grouping

*   Combining multiple shapes to form a new shape.
*   Example: Assembling two triangles to form a square.

    \[ A = T_1 + T_2 + ... + T_n \]

### 6. Paper Folding (Origami)

*   Folds in paper that create creases or edges.
*   Example: Creating a fold with two parallel lines.

    \[ P = \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} x + f_x \\ y + f_y \end{bmatrix} \]

### 7. Cutting and Patterns

*   Shapes created by cutting or removing parts of another shape.
*   Example: Creating a pattern with intersecting lines.

    \[ C = S - R_1 - R_2 ... - R_n \]

### 8. 2D and 3D Transformations

*   Applying transformations in two-dimensional (2D) and three-dimensional (3D) spaces.
*   Example: Rotating a cube around its vertical axis.

    \[ T = \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} x + r_x \\ y + r_y \\ z + r_z \end{bmatrix} \]

**Key Formulas/Theorems**
-------------------------

*   **Translation**: \(T = \begin{bmatrix} x_2 \\ y_2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}x_1 + t_x\\y_1 + t_y\end{bmatrix}\)
*   **Rotation**: \(R = \begin{bmatrix}x'\\y'\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}x \cos(\theta) - y \sin(\theta) \\ x \sin(\theta) + y \cos(\theta)\end{bmatrix}\)
*   **Scaling**: \(S = k \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} kx \\ ky \end{bmatrix}\)

**Problem Solving Patterns**
---------------------------

*   Analyze the problem to identify the type of transformation required.
*   Break down complex transformations into simpler ones.
*   Use formulas and theorems to calculate the result.

**Examples with Solutions**
-------------------------

### Example 1: Translation

A square is translated from (2, 3) to (5, 7). Find the new position.

\[ T = \begin{bmatrix} x_2 \\ y_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 + t_x \\ y_1 + t_y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \]

### Example 2: Rotation

A circle is rotated by 45° clockwise around its center. Find the new position.

\[ R = \begin{bmatrix} x' \\ y' \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} x \cos(\theta) - y \sin(\theta) \\ x \sin(\theta) + y \cos(\theta) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.5x - 0.5y \\ 0.5x + 0.5y \end{bmatrix} \]

### Example 3: Scaling

A rectangle is scaled to twice its size. Find the new dimensions.

\[ S = k \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2x \\ 2y \end{bmatrix} \]

**Common Pitfalls**
------------------

*   Misinterpreting the type of transformation required.
*   Failing to break down complex transformations into simpler ones.
*   Using incorrect formulas or theorems.

**Quick Summary**
-----------------

*   **Translation**: Moving a shape without rotating or scaling it.
*   **Rotation**: Rotating a shape around a fixed point or axis.
*   **Scaling**: Enlarging or shrinking a shape by a certain factor.
*   **Mirroring**: Reflecting a shape across a line or plane.
